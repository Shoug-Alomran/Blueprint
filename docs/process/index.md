# Process

A structured workflow designed to keep projects clear, efficient, and predictable — from initial concept to live deployment.

---

## Delivery Stages

<div class="card-grid hero-cards">

  <article class="hero-card">
    <h3>:one: Discovery</h3>
    <p>Define goals, audience, content readiness, and required pages. Scope is locked before development begins.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="hero-card">
    <h3>:two: Structure</h3>
    <p>Build page hierarchy and navigation flow. Structural layout is approved before visual refinement.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="hero-card">
    <h3>:three: First Build</h3>
    <p>Implement responsive static layout, typography system, and reusable page patterns.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="hero-card">
    <h3>:four: Revisions</h3>
    <p>Refine clarity, organization, and presentation within agreed scope boundaries.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="hero-card">
    <h3>:five: Deployment</h3>
    <p>Publish to production, complete final checks, confirm payment, and transfer ownership.</p>
  </article>

</div>

---

## What This Process Protects

<div class="card-grid">

  <article class="info-card">
    <h3>Scope Control</h3>
    <p>Prevents uncontrolled feature expansion and timeline drift.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="info-card">
    <h3>Quality Assurance</h3>
    <p>Each stage has defined validation before moving forward.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="info-card">
    <h3>Predictable Delivery</h3>
    <p>Clear milestones reduce uncertainty for both sides.</p>
  </article>

</div>

---

## Timeline Notes

- Timeline depends on scope size and content readiness.  
- Delays in content submission or revision feedback may extend delivery.  
- Out-of-scope additions are quoted separately before implementation.  

Structured input leads to faster, cleaner output.
