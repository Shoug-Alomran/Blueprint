# Process

Every Blueprint project follows the same six-stage workflow, from initial fit check to live deployment and ownership transfer.

Each stage has a defined input, a defined output, and a clear condition before moving forward. Nothing is skipped.

---

## Delivery Stages

<div class="card-grid hero-cards">

  <article class="hero-card">
    <h3>01 · Intake & Fit Check</h3>
    <p>Confirm project type, scope, and tier before any work begins.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="hero-card">
    <h3>02 · Discovery</h3>
    <p>Define goals, audience, content readiness, and required sections. Scope is locked here.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="hero-card">
    <h3>03 · Structure & Sitemap</h3>
    <p>Build and approve the navigation hierarchy and content map before any visual work begins.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="hero-card">
    <h3>04 · Build</h3>
    <p>Implement the responsive static layout, typography system, and all deliverables including HTML report conversion if included.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="hero-card">
    <h3>05 · Review & Revisions</h3>
    <p>Refine clarity, organization, and presentation within agreed scope boundaries.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="hero-card">
    <h3>06 · Deployment & Handover</h3>
    <p>Publish to production, run final checks, confirm payment, and transfer ownership.</p>
  </article>

</div>

## Timeline by Tier

| Tier | Scope | Standard Delivery | Priority Delivery |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tier 1 – Personal Presence | Up to 4 sections | 3–5 working days | 2–3 working days |
| Tier 2 – Project Documentation | Up to 8 sections | 5–7 working days | 3–4 working days |
| Tier 3 – Research Documentation | Up to 12 sections | 7–10 working days | 4–6 working days |

Timelines begin after content is submitted and structure is approved. Delays in feedback or content delivery will extend the timeline accordingly.

## What This Process Protects

<div class="card-grid">

  <article class="info-card">
    <h3>Scope Control</h3>
    <p>Each stage is locked before the next begins. Out-of-scope additions are quoted separately before any implementation.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="info-card">
    <h3>Quality Assurance</h3>
    <p>Defined validation conditions at each stage prevent rework later.</p>
  </article>

  <article class="info-card">
    <h3>Predictable Delivery</h3>
    <p>Clear milestones and documented handover reduce uncertainty for both sides.</p>
  </article>

</div>
