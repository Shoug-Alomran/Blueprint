# Revision Policy

To maintain efficiency, fairness, and predictable delivery, all projects include a limited number of revision rounds as defined in the agreed package.

A revision round refers to a single, consolidated set of feedback submitted at one time.

---

## What Counts as a Revision

A revision may include:

- Text edits  
- Minor layout adjustments  
- Color refinements  
- Section rearrangements within existing structure  
- Small structural clarifications  

Revisions are intended to refine — not redesign — the approved structure.

---

## What Is Considered New Scope

The following fall outside revision scope:

- Major layout redesign  
- Adding new pages beyond the agreed package  
- Adding features not previously discussed  
- Changing overall direction after draft delivery  
- Reworking approved sections from scratch  

Such requests may require a revised quote before implementation.

---

## Revision Round Guidelines

- Feedback must be consolidated into a single message per round.  
- Feedback should reference specific sections or pages.  
- Vague requests (e.g., "make it better") may require clarification before changes are made.  
- Additional feedback submitted after a round has been completed may count as a new revision round.

---

## Timeline Impact

- Delays in submitting feedback may extend the project timeline.  
- Projects may be paused if feedback is not received within a reasonable timeframe.

---

## Final Approval

Once final approval is given:

- The project moves to payment.  
- No additional revisions are included.  
- Handover occurs after payment confirmation.  

Structured feedback leads to efficient delivery.
